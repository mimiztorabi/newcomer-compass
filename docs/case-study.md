### The problem

Recent movers often face a dense list of unfamiliar tasks without an obvious first action. Broad web search can increase cognitive load, particularly when local information is incomplete or high stakes.

### Product hypothesis

If a newcomer can choose a broad, low-stakes goal and receive one explained next step, then they may feel more able to begin than when confronted with an unstructured resource list.

### What I designed

- A bounded onboarding journey centered on goal selection.
- An explainable response pattern: suggestion, rationale, checklist, and limitation.
- An AI behavior contract that prioritizes privacy minimization and calibrated uncertainty.
- A no-dependency static prototype that makes the interaction tangible without representing live AI.

### How I would validate the problem

The proposed research plan tests whether choosing the first task is genuinely the hard part, whether guided goals reduce uncertainty, and whether users understand the safety boundaries. It deliberately separates early qualitative learning from later claims about impact or scale. See the [research plan](research-plan.md).

### Key product decisions

| Decision | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Start with selectable goals | Reduces blank-page anxiety and avoids requesting sensitive information. |
| Use prewritten prototype responses | Prevents the demo from making uncontrolled or unsupported claims. |
| Show safety boundaries in the experience | Helps distinguish a planning aid from official or professional guidance. |
| Treat evaluation as a plan, not a result | Keeps the portfolio narrative honest about what has and has not been validated. |
