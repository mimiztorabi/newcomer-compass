# Product brief

<!-- Purpose: frames the user problem, opportunity, audience, and concept before detailed requirements. -->

## One-line concept

Newcomer Compass is a fictional AI-guided planning concept that helps recent movers identify one low-stress, locally relevant next step.

## Problem

Moving creates many unfamiliar tasks at once: finding essential services, understanding local norms, and deciding what to do first. Search results can be broad, inconsistent, and hard to compare. A newcomer may postpone important tasks because the next step feels unclear.

## Target user

**Primary:** an adult who has moved to a new city in the last 90 days and wants practical help settling in.

**Example persona (illustrative):** Maya, 29, moved for work, has limited free time, and wants to prioritize one task this week without sharing sensitive details.

## Opportunity

Use a bounded conversational flow to turn a broad goal into a short checklist, explain why the suggestion fits, and make uncertainty visible. The concept intentionally avoids presenting legal, immigration, medical, financial, or emergency guidance as authoritative.

## Desired outcome

Users leave with a clear next action they can complete independently and the confidence to continue planning.

## Product strategy

The concept focuses on the earliest, lowest-risk moment: turning uncertainty into a manageable action. It does **not** compete on exhaustive local information or automated advice. The strategic bet is that a narrow, explainable planning aid can be more useful—and safer—than an unbounded conversational assistant for this moment.

### Strategic choices

| Choice | Rationale |
| --- | --- |
| Start broad, then narrow | A user can choose a goal without revealing a detailed personal situation. |
| Offer one next step | A short recommendation reduces cognitive load and makes follow-through easier to evaluate. |
| Make verification part of the flow | Local information changes; users should retain control over what they act on. |
| Keep the concept non-transactional | Avoids incentives to steer users toward services, partners, or paid outcomes. |

## Success signals — TODO: validate

- A user can state their goal and select a next action in under five minutes.
- Users report that the explanation is understandable and appropriately cautious.
- Reviewers find that safety boundaries are clear for sensitive requests.

## Non-goals

- Replacing official local services or professional advice.
- Collecting user profiles or providing personalized eligibility decisions.
- Building a live recommendation engine or marketplace.
