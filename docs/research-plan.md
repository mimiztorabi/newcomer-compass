# Research plan

## What needs to be learned

This is a proposed plan, not completed research. It is designed to test the problem framing before a team invests in live AI, local-data integrations, or personalization.

| Assumption to test | Why it matters | Proposed method | Signal to look for |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Newcomers feel blocked by choosing a first task, not only by finding information. | Confirms that a “next step” product is worth exploring. | Five semi-structured interviews with recent movers. | Participants describe prioritization or confidence as a meaningful barrier. |
| Goal cards reduce blank-page anxiety. | Validates the prototype’s guided entry point. | Moderated usability test comparing goal cards with an open prompt. | Participants start more quickly and can explain their choice. |
| A short rationale creates appropriate trust. | Helps avoid both under-trust and over-reliance. | Ask participants what they would do, verify, and avoid after reading a suggestion. | Participants identify the suggestion as a starting point, not authority. |
| Safety language is visible at the moment it is needed. | Protects users who bring high-stakes needs. | Scenario-based comprehension test with sensitive-topic prompts. | Participants choose official or qualified help for high-stakes scenarios. |

## Proposed participant guardrails

- Recruit adults who self-identify as having moved recently; do not require them to disclose immigration status, health, finances, or exact address.
- Explain that the prototype is fictional and does not provide local services or professional advice.
- Allow participants to skip any question and avoid recording identifiable details in notes.

## Interview guide — TODO: tailor before use

1. Tell me about how you decided what to do first after moving.
2. What made a task feel easy or difficult to start?
3. Choose a goal in this prototype. What do you expect to happen next?
4. Read the suggested step aloud in your own words. What would you verify before acting?
5. If your question involved legal, immigration, medical, or financial concerns, what would you expect this product to do?
